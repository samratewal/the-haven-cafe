# The Haven Café — Website Presentation Script

## Before the Meeting

### What to bring
- Laptop or tablet (open the website at thehavencafe.vercel.app or your deployed URL)
- Make sure the website is loaded and working before you walk in
- Have dark mode ready to toggle (shows you thought of everything)

### What to wear
- Smart casual — clean, presentable, like you're visiting a café as a customer but professional

---

## The Presentation (10–15 minutes)

### Step 1: The Hook (30 seconds)

> "Hi, I'm Sam. I'm a local web developer and I actually built a website for The Haven Café — I love this place and thought it deserved a proper online presence. Can I take 5 minutes to show you what I've created?"

**Tip:** Be confident but friendly. Don't oversell — let the website do the talking.

---

### Step 2: First Impression — The Hero (1 minute)

*Open the website and let them see the homepage load*

> "So this is the homepage. Right away you've got the café name, a beautiful background photo, and your location and opening hours. Two clear buttons — 'Explore the Menu' and 'Find Us' — so customers know exactly what to do."

**What to point out:**
- The café name in large, professional text
- The location badge ("Newhaven, Edinburgh · Mon–Fri 8:30–4 · Sat–Sun 9–4")
- The two buttons

**Tip:** Don't scroll yet. Let them take in the first screen.

---

### Step 3: The Story — About Section (1 minute)

*Scroll down slowly to the About section*

> "This is your story section — who you are, what makes The Haven special. It talks about the Scandinavian café culture, the harbour location, and the care that goes into everything. I also added four value cards — Made with Care, Local & Sustainable, Community First, and Quality Always. This is what builds trust with new customers."

**What to point out:**
- The text flows naturally and tells the café's story
- The value cards show what makes them different
- The "Meet the team" button links to the team section

---

### Step 4: The Menu (2 minutes)

*Scroll to the menu section*

> "This is the full menu. It's organised into tabs — Breakfast, Hot Drinks, Cold Drinks, and Extras. Every item has a price, description, and dietary tags. Vegetarian items are marked, and add-ons like bacon or avocado are listed with prices. Customers can see everything before they walk in."

*Click through the tabs to show them*

> "And if someone has a question that's not on the menu, there's a note at the bottom about allergies and dietary requirements."

**What to point out:**
- Click each tab (Breakfast, Hot Drinks, Cold Drinks, Extras) to show it works
- The vegetarian tags (🌱)
- The add-on prices (Bacon +£1.50, etc.)
- The allergy note at the bottom

**Tip:** This is the section they'll care about most. Let them read it.

---

### Step 5: The Gallery (1 minute)

*Scroll to the gallery*

> "This is the gallery — a nice visual peek inside the café. The layout is a bento grid, which is a modern design style that makes the photos look professional and organised. Each photo animates in as you scroll, and they zoom slightly when you hover."

**What to point out:**
- Hover over a photo to show the zoom effect
- The layout looks clean and professional

---

### Step 6: What We Offer (1 minute)

*Scroll to the features section*

> "This section tells customers everything they can expect — organised into four categories: Food & Drink, Dietary Options, Facilities, and Payments. So if someone is wondering 'Do they have vegan options?' or 'Is there parking?', they can see it instantly without calling."

**What to point out:**
- The four categories
- The tags (Vegetarian, Vegan, Gluten-Free, Free WiFi, Wheelchair Access, etc.)

---

### Step 7: Instagram (30 seconds)

*Scroll to the Instagram section*

> "This links straight to your Instagram. If you have one, customers can follow you and tag their photos. It's a great way to build a community and get free marketing."

**What to point out:**
- The Instagram icon with the gradient colours
- The "Visit our page" button

---

### Step 8: Location & Opening Hours (1 minute)

*Scroll to the Find Us section*

> "This is the location section. There's an embedded Google Map so customers can see exactly where you are. Your full address, a 'Get Directions' button that opens Google Maps on their phone, your opening hours for every day of the week, and transport info — how to get here by car, bus, tram, or walking."

**What to point out:**
- The Google Map (it's interactive — they can zoom and pan)
- The "Get Directions" button
- The opening hours table
- The transport options

---

### Step 9: Reviews (1 minute)

*Scroll to the reviews section*

> "This is the reviews section. It shows your overall rating — 4.7 out of 5 — with a breakdown of how many people rated you Excellent, Good, etc. Then individual category scores for Food, Service, Value, and Atmosphere. And below that, real customer testimonials with names, dates, and whether they came from Google or TripAdvisor."

**What to point out:**
- The 4.7 rating
- The breakdown bars
- The category scores (Food 4.6, Service 4.6, Value 4.7, Atmosphere 4.6)
- The testimonial cards

---

### Step 10: FAQ (1 minute)

*Scroll to the FAQ section*

> "This is a FAQ section — answers to the questions customers ask most often. Vegetarian options, vegan meals, gluten-free, WiFi, wheelchair access, card payments, takeaway, parking, and dogs. Each one expands when you click it."

*Click on a couple of questions to show them*

**What to point out:**
- Click 2–3 questions to show the expand animation
- This saves the café staff time answering the same questions on the phone

---

### Step 11: The Team (1 minute)

*Scroll to the team section*

> "And here's the team — Fiona, Calum, Ailsa, and Rory. Each person has their role and a short bio. This makes the café feel personal and welcoming. People like knowing who's making their coffee."

**What to point out:**
- The team member cards with roles and bios

---

### Step 12: Contact (30 seconds)

*Scroll to the contact section*

> "Finally, the contact section. Customers can send a message directly through the website, or they can call, email, or visit. Everything they need is right here."

---

### Step 13: Dark Mode (30 seconds)

*Click the moon/sun icon in the top right*

> "Oh, and one more thing — the website has a dark mode. Just click this icon. The colours change to a warm espresso theme that's easy on the eyes in the evening. It also works perfectly on mobile phones."

**What to point out:**
- Toggle dark mode on and off
- The warm colour scheme

---

### Step 14: Mobile Responsive (30 seconds)

*If you have your phone, open the website on it, or resize the browser window*

> "The website is fully responsive — it looks just as good on a phone as it does on a computer. Most people will find your website on their phone, so this is really important."

---

### Step 15: The Close (1 minute)

> "So that's the website. It's live right now — anyone can visit it. It's fast, it's professional, and it's designed to turn visitors into customers. The menu, the reviews, the location, the gallery — everything a customer needs to decide to visit The Haven Café.

> I built this because I genuinely love this place and I think more people should know about it. If you'd like to use this as your official website, I can set it up with your own domain name — like thehavencafe.co.uk — and make any changes you want. I can update the menu, swap photos, change prices, add new sections — whatever you need.

> What do you think?"

---

## After the Meeting

### If they say YES:
- Ask what they'd like to change (photos, prices, text, etc.)
- Ask about getting a domain name
- Ask if they want a booking/reservation feature
- Set up their custom domain
- Give them your contact details for future updates

### If they say MAYBE:
- Ask what concerns they have
- Offer to make changes based on their feedback
- Leave your contact details
- Follow up in a few days

### If they say NO:
- Thank them for their time
- Ask if you can use it as a portfolio piece
- Leave your contact details — they might change their mind
- Move on to the next café

---

## Quick Tips

1. **Let them hold the laptop/tablet** — they'll feel more in control
2. **Don't rush** — pause after each section, let them react
3. **Ask questions** — "Do you get asked about parking a lot?" (then show the FAQ)
4. **Be honest** — if they ask about something the website doesn't have, say "I can add that"
5. **Show, don't tell** — scroll and click, let them see it working
6. **Smile** — you're showing them something you're proud of

Good luck! You've built something genuinely impressive. 🎉
